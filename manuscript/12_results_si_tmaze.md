# T-maze active-inference rollout {#sec:results_si_tmaze}

<!-- sheaf-track:prose -->

The T-maze rollout instantiates the on-policy student of the thesis: an agent that generates its own observations and acts to minimise expected free energy, exactly as a student policy fitted by on-policy distillation produces its own rollouts and is scored against a teacher at every visited state [@agarwal2024gkd; @parr2022active]. Sophisticated inference here plays the role of beliefs-about-beliefs that newer self-distillation methods exploit when conditioning a teacher on the student's own verified traces or internal predictive signals [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg; @liu2026oisd]. The pymdp harness rolls out the full TMaze active-inference agent under the canonical `{{pymdp_planner}}` planner with SI search horizon {{si_tmaze_planning_horizon}} and Agent policy length {{si_tmaze_policy_len}}. Summary metrics land in `output/data/si_tmaze_summary.json`; trace-level $q_\pi$ rows, action marginals, modality observations, and tree metadata land in `output/data/si_tmaze_trace.json`.

In this correspondence the cue observation is the privileged information $I$ that is available during training but not guaranteed at inference, and `cue validity` quantifies how privileged it is — the analogue of a hint, verified trace, or rich textual feedback in privileged-information and self-distillation settings [@vapnik2009lupi; @lopezpaz2016unifying; @hubotter2026sdpo; @shenfeld2026sdft]. Acting to disambiguate the cue is the epistemic component of expected free energy: the on-policy student seeks the teacher signal precisely on the novel states it reaches itself, which is the information-gain term that off-policy, teacher-generated data cannot supply and which underlies exposure-bias failure [@ross2011dagger; @bengio2015scheduled; @hinton2015distilling; @friston2010fep; @millidge2020efe]. Belief entropy over the rollout is traced in [@fig:si_belief_entropy_curve]; modality-specific observations and selected actions are in [@fig:si_obs_action_trace]. The initial first-action marginal favors the cue-directed action with probability {{si_tmaze_first_action_cue_probability:.3f}}, and [@fig:si_tmaze_actions] shows how $q_\pi$ action probabilities evolve across the rollout.

This single-agent rollout has a direct two-agent reading that we make explicit in the executable classroom demonstration: a privileged teacher with cue validity {{classroom_teacher_cue_validity}} against an on-policy student with cue validity {{classroom_student_cue_validity}} yields teacher belief entropy {{classroom_teacher_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats versus the student's {{classroom_student_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats, demonstrating the privileged-information advantage of the Markov-blanket asymmetry in this toy system. The mean reverse-KL distillation signal between the two is {{classroom_mean_reverse_kl_formatted}} nats — the per-token mode-seeking objective that maps onto variational free energy $F = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\|\,p) - \log p(o)$ [@gu2024minillm; @levine2018rlinference]. The classroom artifact is also where the manuscript keeps internal self-distillation separate from external privileged-information distillation: OISD-style internal alignment [@liu2026oisd] is cited as a method-family analogue, while this figure's numbers come only from `output/data/firstprinciples/classroom.json`. Beyond the static T-maze, a family of dynamic simulators stresses each leg of the correspondence under controlled distribution shift, and their convergent behaviour is what the thesis predicts if reverse-KL distillation truly is variational free-energy minimisation under self-generated observations. The first simulator isolates the *active-sampling* leg: a generalised-knowledge-distillation (GKD) sweep that scores the same teacher signal on student-generated versus teacher-generated rollouts [@agarwal2024gkd]. On-policy GKD attains loss {{gkd_on_policy_loss:.3f}} nats against off-policy {{gkd_off_policy_loss:.3f}} nats, an exposure gap of {{gkd_exposure_gap:.3f}} nats — the strictly positive penalty that off-policy data pays for scoring the teacher off the student's own state-visitation measure, i.e. the missing epistemic (information-gain) term of expected free energy that only on-policy rollouts can resolve [@ross2011dagger; @bengio2015scheduled; @millidge2020efe]. This exposure gap is the simulator-level echo of the cue-disambiguation dynamics traced in [@fig:si_belief_entropy_curve]: the student must sample to be scored where it actually goes.

The second simulator isolates the *free-energy-descent* leg. Treating reverse-KL distillation as variational expectation-maximisation — E-step over the student's own rollout posterior, M-step on the generative-model parameters — the loop converges in {{em_iterations}} steps to a residual variational gap of {{em_final_gap:.3f}} nats, and the descent is monotone ({{em_monotone_descent}}), exactly the non-increasing free-energy trajectory the active-inference account requires when the variational posterior generates its own observations [@friston2010fep; @dacosta2020discrete; @levine2018rlinference]. Monotone descent is the falsifiable signature here: a self-generating-observation objective that is genuinely variational free energy cannot increase across a clean E/M alternation, and the simulator does not.

The third simulator isolates the *risk-tilting* leg through a diversity Pass@k sweep, where the temperature of the student's sampling distribution trades mode-seeking sharpness against coverage — the EFE risk/ambiguity balance read off generation diversity. The flattest (high-entropy, coverage-favouring) student reaches Pass@k {{diversity_flattest_pass_at_k:.3f}} versus the sharpest (low-entropy, mode-collapsed) student at {{diversity_sharpest_pass_at_k:.3f}}, while greedy decoding bottoms out at Pass@1 {{diversity_greedy_pass_at_1:.3f}}; the ordering confirms that the reverse-KL mode-seeking pressure of pure free-energy minimisation must be tempered by an epistemic/coverage term to avoid the collapse that EFE's ambiguity component explicitly penalises [@gu2024minillm; @hinton2015distilling]. Finally, an adaptive-divergence controller that interpolates between reverse- and forward-KL settles at a reverse-fraction of {{adaptive_reverse_fraction:.2f}}, an empirical compromise between the mode-seeking and mass-covering geometries reported separately in this manuscript and consistent with the variational/EFE blend rather than either extreme [@penaloza2026pidistill; @parr2022active]. Each of these four simulator legs is a stylized minimal-model demonstration, not a production-scale measurement; their value is that all four move in the direction the formal correspondence predicts.

We read these results as a minimal-model demonstration of the formal correspondence between on-policy distillation and active inference, not as claims about production language models; the quantitative findings are limited to the analytical toy, the T-maze rollout, the dynamic simulators, and the classroom artifact reported here [@penaloza2026pidistill; @dacosta2020discrete].
