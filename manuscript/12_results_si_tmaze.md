# T-maze active-inference rollout {#sec:results_si_tmaze}

<!-- sheaf-track:prose -->

The T-maze rollout instantiates the on-policy student of the thesis: an agent that generates its own observations and acts to minimise expected free energy, exactly as a student policy fitted by on-policy distillation produces its own rollouts and is scored against a teacher at every visited state [@agarwal2024gkd; @parr2022active]. Sophisticated inference here plays the role of beliefs-about-beliefs that newer self-distillation methods exploit when conditioning a teacher on the student's own verified traces or internal predictive signals [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg; @liu2026oisd]. The pymdp harness rolls out the full TMaze active-inference agent under the canonical `{{pymdp_planner}}` planner with SI search horizon {{si_tmaze_planning_horizon}} and Agent policy length {{si_tmaze_policy_len}}. Summary metrics land in `output/data/si_tmaze_summary.json`; trace-level $q_\pi$ rows, action marginals, modality observations, and tree metadata land in `output/data/si_tmaze_trace.json`.

In this correspondence the cue observation is the privileged information $I$ that is available during training but not guaranteed at inference, and `cue validity` quantifies how privileged it is — the analogue of a hint, verified trace, or rich textual feedback in privileged-information and self-distillation settings [@vapnik2009lupi; @lopezpaz2016unifying; @hubotter2026sdpo; @shenfeld2026sdft]. Acting to disambiguate the cue is the epistemic component of expected free energy: the on-policy student seeks the teacher signal precisely on the novel states it reaches itself, which is the information-gain term that off-policy, teacher-generated data cannot supply and which underlies exposure-bias failure [@ross2011dagger; @bengio2015scheduled; @hinton2015distilling; @friston2010fep; @millidge2020efe]. Rollout transitions: {{si_tmaze_steps}}; recorded timesteps: {{si_tmaze_rollout_timestep_count}}. Mean belief entropy: {{si_tmaze_mean_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats. Belief entropy over the rollout is traced in [@fig:si_belief_entropy_curve]; modality-specific observations and selected actions are in [@fig:si_obs_action_trace]. The initial first-action marginal favors the cue-directed action with probability {{si_tmaze_first_action_cue_probability:.3f}}, and [@fig:si_tmaze_actions] shows how $q_\pi$ action probabilities evolve across the rollout.

This single-agent rollout has a direct two-agent reading that we make explicit in the executable classroom demonstration: a privileged teacher with cue validity {{classroom_teacher_cue_validity}} against an on-policy student with cue validity {{classroom_student_cue_validity}} yields teacher belief entropy {{classroom_teacher_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats versus the student's {{classroom_student_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats, demonstrating the privileged-information advantage of the Markov-blanket asymmetry in this toy system. The mean reverse-KL distillation signal between the two is {{classroom_mean_reverse_kl_formatted}} nats — the per-token mode-seeking objective that maps onto variational free energy $F = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\|\,p) - \log p(o)$ [@gu2024minillm; @levine2018rlinference]. The classroom artifact is also where the manuscript keeps internal self-distillation separate from external privileged-information distillation: OISD-style internal alignment [@liu2026oisd] is cited as a method-family analogue, while this figure's numbers come only from `output/data/firstprinciples/classroom.json`. The matrix/value audit ([@fig:si_tmaze_model_matrices]) confirms {{si_tmaze_matrix_shape_summary}}, normalized A/B/D probability mass, cue validity {{si_tmaze_cue_validity}}, and reward condition {{si_tmaze_reward_condition}}. Policy-comparison rows: {{si_policy_comparison_run_count}} across canonical SI and vanilla comparison-only planners; goal-reaching rows: {{si_policy_comparison_goal_reached_count}}. Graph-world extension rows: {{si_graph_world_steps}} over {{si_graph_world_node_count}} nodes, with goal-reached flag {{si_graph_world_goal_reached}}.

We read these results as a minimal-model demonstration of the formal correspondence between on-policy distillation and active inference, not as claims about production language models; the quantitative findings are limited to the analytical toy, the T-maze rollout, and the classroom artifact reported here [@penaloza2026pidistill; @dacosta2020discrete].
