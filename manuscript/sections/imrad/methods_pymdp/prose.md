**The on-policy student as sophisticated inference (planning horizon).** Under our thesis, on-policy distillation is the student policy acting to minimise expected free energy: it generates its own observations through rollouts and is scored against a privileged target. This section documents the canonical **pymdp full TMaze sophisticated-inference validation profile** ([@fig:tmaze_schematic]) as the executable minimal model of that on-policy student, with SI search horizon {{si_tmaze_planning_horizon}}, rollout length {{si_tmaze_steps}}, and Agent `policy_len = {{si_tmaze_policy_len}}`. Sophisticated inference — beliefs about beliefs over the planning horizon — is precisely the structure invoked by self-distillation methods that condition a teacher on the student's own verified traces [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg], and the agent that rolls out and then minimises expected free energy is the active-sampling counterpart of student rollouts in on-policy distillation [@agarwal2024gkd]. The discrete-state active-inference framing follows finite-POMDP treatments in the synthesis and tutorial literature [@dacosta2020discrete; @smith2022tutorial; @parr2022active], with the expected-free-energy decomposition into epistemic and pragmatic terms supplying the information-gain-versus-reward split that distillation losses recapitulate [@millidge2020efe; @friston2019generalised]. The implementation anchor is pymdp's `TMaze`, `Agent`, `si_policy_search`, and `rollout` APIs [@pymdp2024]. The configured profile is `{{pymdp_profile}}`; the canonical planner is `{{pymdp_planner}}`.

The generative process has {{si_tmaze_location_state_count}} location states/actions, {{si_tmaze_reward_location_state_count}} reward-location states, and {{si_tmaze_observation_modality_count}} observation modalities (location, outcome, cue). The cue modality carries the privileged information of the distillation correspondence: it is the hint, verified trace, or feedback channel available in training but not guaranteed at inference, and `cue_validity` is the strength of that privilege [@vapnik2009lupi; @lopezpaz2016unifying]. That makes the asymmetry operational: the teacher has privileged sensory access, while the on-policy student must act to sample the channel and then be evaluated on the trajectory it actually induced. The model/value audit in `output/data/si_tmaze_model_matrices.json` records {{si_tmaze_matrix_shape_summary}}, dependencies, preferences, deterministic D priors, reward condition {{si_tmaze_reward_condition}}, and cue validity {{si_tmaze_cue_validity}} ([@fig:si_tmaze_model_matrices]). Per-step trace records include $q_\pi$, marginal first-action probabilities, selected action names, modality-specific observations, belief entropy, and SI tree metadata — the on-policy trajectory the student writes and is then scored against.

Graph-world artifacts are deterministic extension outputs declared in `tracks.yaml` `extension_tracks.graph_world`. For the reference workflow, see [@sec:intro_motivation]; measured rollouts appear in [@sec:results_si_tmaze].

Mean belief entropy across recorded timesteps: {{si_tmaze_mean_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats. Because the cue is informative, the student that acts to observe it drives its posterior entropy down — the epistemic value of seeking privileged information made quantitative. The initial $q_\pi$ first-action marginal assigns probability {{si_tmaze_first_action_cue_probability:.3f}} to the cue-directed action, which is the canonical first-action argmax under the configured SI search: the on-policy student elects, from its own beliefs, to sample the privileged channel first.

The comparison artifact `output/data/si_policy_comparison.json` runs the canonical SI planner and a vanilla pymdp planner as validation rows only; vanilla is marked `{{si_policy_comparison_vanilla_role}}` and never replaces the canonical summary. It records {{si_policy_comparison_run_count}} deterministic comparison rows, complete-grid flag {{si_policy_comparison_complete_grid}}, and {{si_policy_comparison_goal_reached_count}} goal-reaching rows under the same full TMaze transition model. We read this contrast as the difference between an agent that minimises expected free energy on-policy and a myopic baseline. That contrast is the active-inference image of the induced-distribution issue from imitation learning and sequence generation: the learner must be trained on states it actually causes, not only on teacher-generated states [@ross2011dagger; @bengio2015scheduled; @hinton2015distilling; @agarwal2024gkd].

Agent construction and rollout diagnostics are audited in `output/reports/pymdp_runtime_diagnostics.json`: {{pymdp_runtime_construction_count}} constructions, {{pymdp_runtime_known_warning_count}} known third-party JAX static-array warnings, {{si_tree_known_max_node_warning_count}} known SI tree max-node diagnostics, and {{pymdp_runtime_unexpected_warning_count}} unexpected warnings. Policy posterior evidence is written separately to `output/data/pymdp_policy_posterior_grid.json` with {{pymdp_policy_posterior_row_count}} rows and normalized-posterior flag {{pymdp_policy_posteriors_normalized}}.

The graph-world extension is deterministic: `simulate_si_graph_world.py` writes `si_graph_world_summary.json` and `si_graph_world_trace.json` for a four-node graph-world path. The regenerated summary reports {{si_graph_world_node_count}} nodes, {{si_graph_world_steps}} steps, and goal-reached flag {{si_graph_world_goal_reached}}. The topology-trace extension records {{si_graph_world_topology_trace_count}} topology traces with agreement flag {{si_graph_world_topology_traces_agree}}. As with the analytical toy, these are a minimal-model demonstration of the on-policy-distillation/active-inference correspondence — claims are limited to these pymdp models and artifacts, not to production LLM systems.
