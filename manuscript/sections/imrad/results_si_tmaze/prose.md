The T-maze rollout instantiates the on-policy student of the thesis: an agent that generates its own observations and acts to minimise expected free energy, exactly as a student policy fitted by on-policy distillation produces its own rollouts and is scored against a teacher at every visited state [@agarwal2024gkd; @friston2017process; @parr2022active; @vanoostrum2024discrete_active_inference]. Sophisticated inference here plays the role of beliefs-about-beliefs that newer self-distillation methods exploit when conditioning a teacher on the student's own verified traces or internal predictive signals [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg; @liu2026oisd]. Beyond the static T-maze, a family of dynamic simulators stresses each leg of the correspondence under controlled distribution shift. Their convergent behaviour is what the thesis predicts if reverse-KL distillation truly is variational free-energy minimisation under self-generated observations. The first simulator isolates the *active-sampling* leg: a generalised-knowledge-distillation (GKD) sweep that scores the same teacher signal on student-generated versus teacher-generated rollouts [@agarwal2024gkd]. Scoring the same student under its own state-visitation measure reveals {{gkd_on_policy_loss:.3f}} nats of loss, of which a teacher-visitation evaluation sees only {{gkd_off_policy_loss:.3f}} nats — an exposure gap of {{gkd_exposure_gap:.3f}} nats that off-policy evaluation hides, i.e. the epistemic (information-gain) term of expected free energy that only on-policy rollouts can expose [@ross2011dagger; @sun2017aggrevated; @bengio2015scheduled; @arora2022exposure; @rohatgi2025next_token_barrier; @pozzi2025exposure_distill; @millidge2020efe]. This exposure gap is the simulator-level echo of the cue-disambiguation dynamics traced in [@fig:si_belief_entropy_curve]: the student must sample to be scored where it actually goes.

The second simulator isolates the *free-energy-descent* leg. Treating reverse-KL distillation as variational expectation-maximisation - E-step over the student's own rollout posterior, M-step on the generative-model parameters - the loop converges in {{em_iterations}} steps to a residual variational gap of {{em_final_gap:.3f}} nats, and the descent is monotone ({{em_monotone_descent}}), exactly the non-increasing free-energy trajectory expected under this clean E/M alternation [@friston2010fep; @friston2017process; @dacosta2020discrete; @levine2018rlinference; @fellows2018virel]. The falsifiable signature here is local and algorithmic: this exact toy E/M objective should not increase across its own updates. It is not a claim that stochastic OPD training is globally monotone under arbitrary optimizers, teacher targets, or rollout distributions.

The third simulator isolates the *risk-tilting* leg through a diversity Pass-at-k sweep, in which the temperature of the student's sampling distribution trades finite-sample sharpness against coverage - the EFE risk/ambiguity balance read off generation diversity. For independent samples, $Pass@k = 1-(1-p)^k$, so the curve reports how the probability of at least one correct sample changes as the student distribution flattens or sharpens. The flattest (high-entropy, coverage-favouring) student reaches Pass-at-k {{diversity_flattest_pass_at_k:.3f}} ([@fig:diversity_tradeoff]) versus the sharpest (low-entropy, concentrated) student at {{diversity_sharpest_pass_at_k:.3f}}, while greedy decoding bottoms out at Pass-at-1 {{diversity_greedy_pass_at_1:.3f}}; the ordering shows why a pure concentration objective can need an epistemic/coverage term in finite tasks [@gu2024minillm; @hinton2015distilling; @wu2024rethinking_kl_kd; @stanton2021kd_work; @jin2026entropy_opd]. Finally, an adaptive-divergence controller that interpolates between reverse- and forward-KL settles at a reverse-fraction of {{adaptive_reverse_fraction:.2f}}, an empirical compromise between concentration and mass-covering geometries reported separately in this manuscript and consistent with the variational/EFE blend rather than either extreme [@ko2024distillm; @ko2025distillm2; @zhu2026hpd; @penaloza2026pidistill; @parr2022active]. Each of these four simulator legs is a stylized minimal-model demonstration, not a production-scale measurement; their value is that all four move in the direction the formal correspondence predicts.

We read these results as a minimal-model demonstration of the formal correspondence between on-policy distillation and active inference, not as claims about production language models; the quantitative findings are limited to the analytical toy, the T-maze rollout, the dynamic simulators, and the classroom artifact reported here [@penaloza2026pidistill; @dacosta2020discrete].
