# Free-energy decomposition {#sec:results_free_energy}

<!-- sheaf-track:prose -->

Read as a distillation-objective landscape, the variational free energy of the student against the reward-tilted teacher target is evaluated along the same $\lambda$ grid used for the MI sweep ([@fig:free_energy_curve]), where $\lambda$ couples the teacher's privileged variable to the answer and thus sets how much teacher–student mutual information the objective must absorb. [@fig:free_energy_curve] separates two objects that are easy to conflate. Against the *entangled* target $p_\lambda$ — the analogue of the teacher policy $\pi_T(y\mid x, I)$ that carries privileged information $I$ — the entangled posterior $q_\lambda$ is evaluated against its own normalized target, so $F(q_\lambda;p_\lambda)=0$ to numerical tolerance (maximum absolute value {{free_energy_exact_target_max_abs:.1e}} nats). That zero is not an absence of structure; it is the finite self-distillation limit [@zhao2026opsd] where the tractable student family has exactly recovered the target it is fit against and the reverse-KL loss vanishes.

The decomposition implemented in `src/analytical/decomposition.py` then splits that zero into per-stream marginal free energies, a coupling-cost term, a coupling-prior term, and a total-correlation gain — the same epistemic/pragmatic ledger that expected-free-energy methods balance in active inference [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active; @millidge2020efe; @champion2024efe_unification; @sajid2021bayesian_design]. For the symmetric toy with uniform marginals, the coupling-prior term equals $-I(\lambda)$ and exactly cancels the total-correlation gain $+I(\lambda)$; the merged invariant suite checks this cancellation directly ({{invariants_passed}}/{{invariants_total}} pass). This is the precise sense in which teacher–student mutual information is the slack between the coupled target and the factorized family for this model.

The nonzero curve in [@fig:free_energy_curve] is the free-energy gap against the *mean-field* independent prior $q_0$, which plays the role of the mode-covering, forward-KL SFT baseline [@hinton2015distilling] that ignores the privileged coupling. Its minimum at $\lambda={{free_energy_argmin_lambda}}$ occurs where the entangled posterior coincides with the factorized mean-field product; any $\lambda>0$ raises the gap as coupling pulls the posterior away from that independent prior. At the configured sweep maximum the gap reaches {{free_energy_mean_field_gap_max:.3f}} nats and equals mutual information up to {{free_energy_gap_equals_mi_max_abs:.1e}} nats, so the rising branch is the information gap the on-policy reverse-KL objective must close [@gu2024minillm; @agarwal2024gkd]. As the privileged variable becomes more diagnostic, a student that cannot condition on $I$ pays an increasing cost, exactly the regime where on-policy self-distillation toward a teacher conditioned on verified traces is designed to operate [@liu2026sdpg; @hubotter2026sdpo].

Saturation MI (grid maximum on the measured $\lambda$ sweep): {{ising_mi_saturation}} nats. This is the largest coupling attained on the measured $\lambda$ grid, not the model's ceiling: as $\lambda\to\infty$ the mutual information saturates toward $\log 2 \approx 0.693$ nats ([@eq:mutual_information]), which is the information the privileged target can carry beyond the student's reach in this binary toy. As throughout, the claim is limited to this analytical toy as a faithful minimal demonstration of the correspondence, not an assertion about production LLMs.

### Energy decompositions: VFE and EFE {#sec:energy_decompositions}

To make the correspondence between the reverse-KL distillation loss and variational free energy (VFE) explicit, we evaluate the two energy ledgers of active inference on the same minimal model and report them in [@fig:energy_decomposition]. VFE admits the standard complexity-minus-accuracy reading [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active]: $F = \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}[q(s)\,\|\,p(s)]}_{\text{complexity}} - \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_q[\ln p(o\mid s)]}_{\text{accuracy term}}$, equivalently the negative log-evidence offset by the posterior-to-true-posterior gap. In the prior-evaluated panel, the complexity term is {{energy_complexity:.3f}} nats because $q(s)=p(s)$, so the KL cost of moving off the prior is zero. The accuracy term is {{energy_accuracy:.3f}} nats because it is an expected log likelihood and is negative under this observation model; therefore the plotted VFE is $0 - ({{energy_accuracy:.3f}})$, the positive surprisal term behind the reported log-evidence bound {{energy_log_evidence:.3f}} nats. The point is not that energy disappears, but that the cost comes entirely from insufficient accuracy when the student remains at the prior. In distillation language, that is the unlearned teacher signal: minimizing $F$ tightens the reverse KL between the tractable student $\pi_S(y\mid x)$ and the privileged target $\pi_T(y\mid x, I)$ up to the model-evidence constant $-\ln p(o)$ that is invariant to the student parameters [@levine2018rlinference; @dacosta2020discrete]. This correspondence is an algebraic objective identity inside the declared finite categorical family — the variational reading of the divergence in the sense of the free-energy-principle mathematical review [@buckley2017mathreview] — so a shared objective form fixes the optimum here but does not carry over to the optimization dynamics or scaling of large-model distillation, where the same reverse-KL objective behaves in support-, parameterisation-, and optimization-dependent ways rather than as a universal law [@wu2024rethinking_kl_kd].

Expected free energy (EFE) extends this ledger forward over the student's own rollouts, which is where the *on-policy* character of distillation enters: the student generates the very observations it is then scored against, so the relevant energy is an expectation over self-generated trajectories rather than a fixed teacher-forced corpus [@devries2025efe_planning_vi; @liu2026sdpg; @hubotter2026sdpo]. EFE splits two equivalent ways. The risk-plus-ambiguity reading assigns {{efe_risk:.3f}} nats to risk (the divergence of predicted from preferred outcomes — the reward-tilting term that biases rollouts toward the verified target) and {{efe_ambiguity:.3f}} nats to ambiguity (the expected observation entropy under the generative mapping). The epistemic-plus-pragmatic reading assigns {{efe_epistemic:.3f}} nats to the epistemic (information-gain) drive and {{efe_pragmatic:.3f}} nats to the pragmatic (goal-seeking) drive. The pragmatic term is the active-inference image of reward-tilting in on-policy distillation, while the epistemic term is the active-sampling pressure that makes on-policy rollouts close the exposure-bias gap the off-policy SFT baseline leaves open [@qwen2025technical_report; @thinkingmachines2025opd; @zhao2026opsd].

The full variational- and expected-free-energy decomposition is tabulated in the supplement ([@sec:appendix_full_sheaf]).

The table makes the thesis quantitative on one model: VFE is the static distillation objective (complexity {{energy_complexity:.3f}} traded against accuracy {{energy_accuracy:.3f}}), and EFE is its on-policy extension, where the pragmatic drive {{efe_pragmatic:.3f}} is reward-tilting and the epistemic drive {{efe_epistemic:.3f}} is active sampling over the student's own rollouts. As elsewhere, these are nats from a faithful minimal-model demonstration, not production measurements; the literature-reported empirics are reported separately.
