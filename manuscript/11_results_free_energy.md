# Free-energy decomposition {#sec:results_free_energy}

<!-- sheaf-track:prose -->

Read as a distillation-objective landscape, the variational free energy of the student against the reward-tilted teacher target is evaluated along the same $\lambda$ grid used for the MI sweep ([@fig:free_energy_curve]), where $\lambda$ couples the teacher's privileged variable to the answer and thus sets how much teacher–student mutual information the objective must absorb. Against the *entangled* prior — the analogue of the teacher policy $\pi_T(y\mid x, I)$ that carries privileged information $I$ — the entangled posterior is the exact variational minimizer, so its free energy is identically zero. This is the finite self-distillation limit [@zhao2026opsd]: the tractable student family $\pi_S(y\mid x)$ has recovered the target it is fit against and the reverse-KL distillation loss vanishes.

The decomposition implemented in `src/analytical/decomposition.py` then splits that zero into per-stream marginal free energies, a coupling-cost term, a coupling-prior term, and a total-correlation gain — the same epistemic/pragmatic ledger that expected-free-energy methods balance in active inference [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active; @millidge2020efe]. For the symmetric toy with uniform marginals, the coupling-prior term equals $-I(\lambda)$ and exactly cancels the total-correlation gain $+I(\lambda)$; the merged invariant suite checks this cancellation directly ({{invariants_passed}}/{{invariants_total}} pass). This is the precise sense in which teacher–student mutual information is the slack between the coupled target and the factorized family for this model.

The curve in [@fig:free_energy_curve] instead reports free energy against the *mean-field* prior, which plays the role of the mode-covering, forward-KL SFT baseline [@hinton2015distilling] that ignores the privileged coupling. Its minimum at $\lambda={{free_energy_argmin_lambda}}$ occurs where the entangled posterior coincides with the factorized mean-field product; any $\lambda>0$ raises the free energy as coupling pulls the posterior away from that independent prior. The rising branch is therefore the distillation gap the on-policy reverse-KL objective must close [@gu2024minillm; @agarwal2024gkd]: as the privileged variable becomes more diagnostic, a student that cannot condition on $I$ pays an increasing free-energy cost, exactly the regime where on-policy self-distillation toward a teacher conditioned on verified traces is designed to operate [@liu2026sdpg; @hubotter2026sdpo].

Saturation MI (grid maximum on the measured $\lambda$ sweep): {{ising_mi_saturation}} nats. This grid maximum is the strongest teacher–student coupling the minimal model can express, and bounds the maximal information the privileged target carries beyond the student's reach; as throughout, the claim is limited to this analytical toy as a faithful minimal demonstration of the correspondence, not an assertion about production LLMs.
