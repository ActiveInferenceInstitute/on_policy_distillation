## Scientific scope

A student policy trained only on a teacher's own outputs learns under a distribution it will not induce at inference time. That is the sequential-prediction failure mode studied in interactive imitation learning [@ross2011dagger] and scheduled sampling [@bengio2015scheduled]: each generated token or action changes the next state distribution, so errors can compound precisely where the learner has not been trained. Passive, off-policy supervised distillation [@hinton2015distilling] inherits that mismatch by minimising a teacher-data objective on trajectories drawn from the teacher rather than the student. On-policy distillation repairs the mismatch by scoring the student's *own* samples under the teacher and minimising a reverse or flexible KL on the induced student distribution [@gu2024minillm; @agarwal2024gkd]. Recent OPD studies sharpen the boundary of that repair: teacher choice, loss formulation, privileged-information type, and long-horizon reward coupling can determine whether the on-policy signal stabilises learning or introduces new failure modes [@zhu2026manyfacesopd; @ramos2026dgrpo]. The same correction is the defining move of active inference: an agent acts to generate the observations under which its approximate posterior is then refined [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active; @dacosta2020discrete].

The paper's thesis is therefore a scoped equivalence of formal objects, not a loose metaphor. In the finite models studied here, the teacher policy plays the role of the intractable generative model $p(o,s)$, the student policy is the tractable approximate posterior $q(s)$, the variational free energy $F = D_{KL}(q\,\|\,p) - \log p(o)$ is the per-token reverse-KL distillation loss, and on-policy student rollouts are the active sampling that lets the posterior generate its own observations. Privileged information available in training but not at inference — a hint, verified trace, feedback channel, or layer-internal predictive signal — is the privileged sensory access on the teacher's side of a Markov blanket, linking learning under privileged information [@vapnik2009lupi], distillation-as-privileged-information [@lopezpaz2016unifying], recent privileged-context self-distillation [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg; @penaloza2026pidistill], and internal on-policy self-distillation [@liu2026oisd].

This manuscript couples three tracks on toy Active Inference models, each a minimal-model demonstration of one face of this correspondence: a Bernoulli–Ising analytical oracle, a pymdp T-maze rollout, and a sheaf-indexed assembly contract that binds {{sheaf_track_count}} optional fragment tracks under an IMRAD outline. The conceptual lineage is the free-energy and active-inference literature [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active], but the scientific claims stay within these models and their generated artifacts — they are not empirical statements about biological agents or production language models.

## Manuscript structure

Three **scientific tracks** (analytical, pymdp, sheaf composition) map onto {{sheaf_track_count}} **composable fragment types** and {{pipeline_track_count}} pipeline gates ([@fig:multi_track_architecture]); each demonstrates one face of the teacher–student correspondence rather than standing as an isolated exhibit. The Bernoulli–Ising analytical oracle is a minimal model of teacher–student coupling: a coupling $\lambda$ ties the teacher's privileged variable to the answer, the mutual information $I(\lambda)$ is the teacher–student mutual information, and the free energy of the entangled posterior against a factorised mean-field prior is the distillation objective — with reverse KL recovering the mode-seeking self-distillation limit and forward KL the mode-covering SFT limit. The pymdp T-maze rollout is the on-policy student itself: an agent that generates its own observations and acts to minimise expected free energy, where the cue is the privileged information and its validity sets how privileged it is. A two-agent classroom simulation closes the loop, running a privileged teacher (cue validity {{classroom_teacher_cue_validity}}) against an on-policy student (cue validity {{classroom_student_cue_validity}}): teacher belief entropy of {{classroom_teacher_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats falls below the student's {{classroom_student_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats, demonstrating the privileged advantage in this toy system, with a mean reverse-KL distillation signal of {{classroom_mean_reverse_kl_formatted}} nats.
