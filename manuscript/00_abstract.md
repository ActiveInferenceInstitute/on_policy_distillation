# Abstract {#sec:abstract}

On-policy distillation admits a scoped active-inference reading — exact for the closed-form objects and interpretive for the on-policy-rollout reading — in the finite models studied here: the variational posterior generates its own observations, and the generative model is conditioned on privileged beliefs. The intractable teacher policy plays the role of the generative model $p(o,s)$, the tractable student policy is the approximate posterior $q(s)$, and the per-token reverse-KL distillation loss is the variational free energy $F = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\|\,p(s\mid o)) - \log p(o)$, whose KL target is the exact posterior $p(s\mid o)\propto p(o,s)$ induced by the teacher; on-policy student rollouts generate the self-induced observations on which that variational objective is evaluated, so the posterior is scored on the observations it caused [@friston2010fep; @parr2022active; @levine2018rlinference]. The active-inference vocabulary distinguishes two objectives, and we keep them apart: the distillation loss instantiates *variational* free energy on realized rollouts, while action selection and planning are more naturally discussed through *expected* free energy — which is how the pymdp agent below chooses its actions [@millidge2020efe; @friston2021sophisticated; @dacosta2020discrete]. This is closely related to — though not identical with — the induced-distribution mismatch studied in interactive imitation learning and parts of the exposure-bias literature, whose objectives and empirical regimes differ and whose severity is itself contested [@ross2011dagger; @bengio2015scheduled; @huszar2015hownot; @ranzato2016sequence; @he2021selfrecovery], with privileged traces and feedback playing the role that train-time-only information plays in the LUPI/distillation lineage [@vapnik2009lupi; @lopezpaz2016unifying]. We instantiate the correspondence in a minimal Active Inference stack of three executable models. A Bernoulli-Ising analytical oracle couples a teacher's privileged variable to the answer through a single parameter $\lambda$: its mutual information $I(\lambda)$ is the teacher-student mutual information, its free energy is the toy distillation objective, and the reverse/forward KL contrast is a finite-support divergence-direction intuition rather than a universal law about LLM training [@hinton2015distilling; @gu2024minillm; @agarwal2024gkd; @wu2024rethinking_kl_kd; @hernandezlobato2016blackbox_alpha; @ke2019f_divergence_imitation]. A pymdp T-maze rollout is the on-policy student: an agent that generates its own observations and acts to minimise expected free energy under a privileged cue [@friston2021sophisticated; @millidge2020efe; @vanoostrum2024discrete_active_inference]. A two-agent classroom run pits a privileged teacher (cue validity {{classroom_teacher_cue_validity}}) against an on-policy student (cue validity {{classroom_student_cue_validity}}), measuring a privileged *entropy* advantage - teacher belief entropy {{classroom_teacher_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats versus student {{classroom_student_belief_entropy_formatted}} nats - and a mean reverse-KL distillation signal of {{classroom_mean_reverse_kl_formatted}} nats, matching the objective structure of recent privileged-context, context-distillation, and adaptive-teacher OPD methods while leaving shortcut privilege and teacher-reliability failures explicit [@zhao2026opsd; @liu2026sdpg; @penaloza2026pidistill; @snell2022context_distillation; @ye2026context_distillation; @lazaridis2026edge_opd; @han2026adaptive_teacher_exposure]. A teacher-privilege sweep with a built-in identical-agent control shows the entropy advantage is strongly nonlinear in teacher privilege, while the reverse-KL signal — the loss itself — rises monotonically above a stated noise floor and registers privilege at lower cue validities than posterior-entropy summaries do. Claims are limited to these models and their generated artifacts: the toy results demonstrate the correspondence rather than asserting anything about production LLMs, where Qwen's OPD-vs-RL table and Thinking Machines' replication context remain external context rather than reproduced results [@qwen2025technical_report; @thinkingmachines2025opd].
